Email Etiquette Guidelines

Following proper email etiquette helps ensure your message is well-received and increases the likelihood of a prompt, helpful response.

{/* Subject line guidelines */}

Subject Line Best Practices

  • Be specific and descriptive: Instead of "Question" use "React Performance Question - Large Component Re-renders"
  • Include the type of inquiry: "Project Inquiry", "Collaboration Request", "Technical Question", etc.
  • Keep it concise: Aim for 50 characters or less to ensure it displays fully on mobile devices
  • Avoid spam triggers: Don't use all caps, excessive punctuation, or words like "URGENT" unless truly necessary
  • Make it actionable: If you need a response by a certain date, include it: "Project Proposal - Response Needed by March 15"
{/* Email structure guidelines */}

Email Structure Guidelines

{/* Opening */}
Opening
  • Use a professional greeting: "Dear John", "Hi John", or "Hello John"
  • Introduce yourself briefly if we haven't communicated before
  • Mention how you found my contact information or work
{/* Body */}
Body
  • Get to the point quickly - state your purpose in the first paragraph
  • Use bullet points or numbered lists for multiple questions or requirements
  • Provide context and relevant details, but keep it concise
  • Include specific information like timelines, budgets, or technical requirements
  • Break up long paragraphs for better readability
{/* Closing */}
Closing
  • Clearly state what you're looking for: a response, meeting, quote, etc.
  • Provide your availability for calls or meetings
  • Thank me for my time and consideration
  • Use a professional sign-off: "Best regards", "Sincerely", "Thank you"
  • Include your full contact information in your signature
{/* Communication preferences */}

My Communication Preferences

  • Response time: I typically respond within 24-48 hours. If your matter is urgent, please mention it in the subject line.
  • Detail level: I appreciate detailed emails that provide context, but please be concise and organized.
  • Attachments: Feel free to include relevant documents, but please mention them in your email body.
  • Follow-ups: If you don't hear back within 48 hours, a polite follow-up is perfectly acceptable.
  • Meeting requests: I prefer to have some context via email before scheduling calls or meetings.
{/* What to avoid */}

What to Avoid

  • Vague requests: "I need a website" without any details about requirements, timeline, or budget
  • Immediate response expectations: Demanding responses within hours unless it's a genuine emergency
  • Overly casual tone: While I'm friendly, maintain professionalism in initial communications
  • Mass emails: Generic emails clearly sent to multiple people without personalization
  • Excessive follow-ups: Multiple emails within 24 hours unless there's a critical update

How quickly do you respond to emails?

I typically respond within 24 hours for business inquiries and within 48 hours for general questions. During weekends or holidays, responses may take longer.

What if my email ends up in spam?

Can I send large files via email?

Email attachments should be under 10MB. For larger files, please use cloud storage services like Google Drive, Dropbox, or WeTransfer and share the link in your email.

Do you accept cold emails?

Yes, I welcome professional cold emails, especially for interesting projects or collaboration opportunities. Just make sure to follow the guidelines above for the best response.

What information should I include for project inquiries?

Include project scope, timeline, budget range, technical requirements, and any specific preferences. The more details you provide, the better I can assess if we're a good fit.

Do you offer free consultations via email?

I'm happy to answer brief questions and provide general guidance via email. For detailed consultations, I prefer to schedule a call or video meeting.
